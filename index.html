Paper + Pixel:

How artists blend physical and digital mediums

Introduction

The ever-advancing of technology has significantly impacted the art industry, in the form of digital art. Artists are increasingly using electronic devices and softwares to create digital art, and consumers can easily access them due to increased internet accessibility and smartphone adoption. The demand and supply of digital art were further accelerated by Covid-19 given the limited access to traditional art, which brought about the rise in popularity of Non-Fungible Tokens (NFTs) (Dickinson 3). NFTs are unique digital files managed on blockchain and purchased through cryptocurrencies (VISA 3). They transformed how art is owned, from having physical certificates of authenticity for traditional art, to smart contracts for digital art. This allows for easier verification and protection of ownership. NFTs can blend traditional and digital art together. They allow traditional art to exist beyond paintings and sculptures, and be turned into 3D and pixel art through digitalisation in the form of NFTs. Digital art can also be made physical and gain tangible elements. For instance, unique artworks like Tom Sach’s “Rocket Factory” have integrated both mediums creatively and seamlessly. Buyers can not only build their own rocket NFTs digitally, but also receive a physical version built by Tom Sach himself. Leveraging on the two mediums reshapes the way art is being produced and distributed, thereby creating more innovative and interactive art (Ferree). The research proposal aims to firstly gain a deeper understanding of NFTs and how modern day artists are integrating traditional and digital art through books, articles and whitepapers. Through these readings, this research aims to find ways with proposed methodologies on creating NFTs that harmoniously blend traditional and digital art together.

Purpose & Rationale

In the ﬁrst half of 2021, NFT sales rose to a record US$2.5 billion (Hill 50). With the rise of the Metaverse, a shared virtual environment by internet users, the physical world is being rapidly replicated into virtual realities. NFT-based digital art would be an important element in supporting these metaverse experiences, thus continuing to play a significant role in transforming the art industry (Shahriar et al 2). Given the exponential growth in digital art, this research proposal aims to review how traditional and digital art mediums can be blended and compliment each other, driven by the growing popularity of NFTs.
